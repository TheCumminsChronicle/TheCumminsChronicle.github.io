Cummins bags second prize at Purushottam Karandak

Written by Aishwarya Lanke of TE IT

If you've passed by the Quadrangle early in the morning these few months, you'd have been mesmerized by the sight that met your eyes. A group of 30-odd girls began their day by reciting Sanskrit shlokas as a part of their voice projection exercises and then set off working till late evening. Kudos to the tireless efforts of the Purushottam Team! They have brought laurels to our college by winning several prizes at the prestigious Marathi drama competition, Purushottam Karandak.

The long journey began with auditions conducted in June. The award-winning script revolves around the story of tribal women in a village of India who are very fascinated by a photographer's camera. They acquire the photography skills, in spite of all the obstacles in their way and went on to shoot the UN Council. After months of script writing, prop making, costume designing and countless rehearsals, the team cleared the first round to emerge in the top 9 colleges amongst the 51 colleges that participated. The team has won trophies for their direction, innovation and acting skills. The team invented a new language and used innovative costumes in the play, which won them the Sarvotkrushta Prayogik Ekankika Karandak or the 'Best Innovative Drama' prize.

A different bond is formed with all the girls as they have become a family, says Anuprita Joshi, the student representative of the Purushottam Team. The team is grateful to all the help and freedom that the college provided them.

We are immensely proud of our Purushottam Team! Many Congratulations again!
