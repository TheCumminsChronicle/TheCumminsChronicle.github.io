Word Buzz

sequester

[si-kwes-ter] | verb

Definition:

  1. to remove or withdraw into solitude or retirement; seclude.

  2. to keep apart from others; segregate or isolate.

Example:

  1. In an attempt to break his own videogame record, Daniel chose to sequester himself in his bedroom for an entire weekend.

  2. The jury was sequestered until a verdict was reached.
